Details

There are several weaknesses in this trained model that make it unreliable.

I used 200 posts from reddit's /r/SuicideWatch and 200 posts from /r/Jokes. Everything from SuicideWatch was labelled as suicidal (1) while all the Jokes posts were labelled as non suicidal (0).

The amount of data should be way higher, and the nature of the data is too unvaried. It's all from reddit and the non suicidal examples only consist of jokes.

The model was first experimented with in Weka, then recreated in Java using Weka's Java libraries. The Multilayer Perceptron classifier came out with some of the best results and 97% accuracy, however my original intention was to use a Naive Bayes classifier.

Take the accuracy with a big grain of salt, because it was tested on the training data. It's normally recommended to have a completely separate dataset dedicated for testing only.

Nevertheless, the results are pretty good although not perfect.

Also take note that what you think might be an example of a suicidal person's statement, might not be something they would actually say in reality and may come out as non-suicidal. The model, although limited, is trained on real examples.
